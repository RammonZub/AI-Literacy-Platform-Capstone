# Module 1 — AI Fundamentals

**Code path:** `src/data/content/modules/01-skills-foundations/`

## Purpose

This module serves as the conceptual entry point to the curriculum. It establishes a foundational mental model for understanding artificial intelligence, sets realistic expectations about AI capabilities, and introduces the tool landscape without advocating for any specific vendor. The target learner is a non-technical professional who may find the volume of AI-related information overwhelming.

## Curriculum Position

Module 1 is the first module in the catalog sequence and has no prerequisites. All subsequent modules — productivity habits, prompt engineering, and tool-specific mastery — assume that the learner has a baseline understanding of what AI is, where it applies to professional work, and how to begin using it.

The personalization algorithm routes learners with no prior AI experience to this module through the beginner override rule (Thesis §4.2.3).

## Pedagogical Approach

Lessons are structured with short, accessible content that avoids technical jargon. The module progresses from conceptual framing through practical use cases to a first repeatable workflow. The final lessons ensure the learner has both the vocabulary and a concrete starting point to continue with subsequent modules.

## Structure

- 4 chapters, 12 lessons, approximately 65 minutes total estimated duration.
- Learning outcomes: mental model of AI, realistic use case identification, tool landscape awareness, first personal workflow.
